# IRON VISUAL CONSTITUTION (IVC-01)
## The Law of Appearance for Sovereign Systems

### PREAMBLE
IRON is not an application. IRON is an institutional operating environment for human self-governance.
Every rendered surface must express: Authority over comfort. Structure over decoration. Permanence over trend.

### ARTICLE I — CHROMATIC ORDER (Color Law)
Color exists to encode institutional hierarchy and system state. No color exists for branding.

#### The Four Chromatic Classes
1.  **INFRASTRUCTURE (The Void)**: Substrate, silence, permanence. (`#0A0D12`).
2.  **STRUCTURE (The Material)**: Containment, separation. Dark graphite.
3.  **SIGNAL (The Law)**: System meaning. Cold Cyan (Active), Crimson (Breach), Viridian (Recovery).
4.  **FIELD (The Light)**: Atmospheric presence. No visible color stops.

### ARTICLE II — LUMINANCE & LIGHT LAW
IRON does not use brightness; it uses depth.
Planes must never visually collapse:
*   **Void Plane**: Infinite background.
*   **Structural Plane**: Panels and chambers.
*   **Signal Plane**: Typography and telemetry.

### ARTICLE III — TYPOGRAPHIC CONSTITUTION
Typography is Jurisdiction.
1.  **CONSTITUTIONAL**: Authority, permanence. Serif (Cinzel, Garamond).
2.  **INSTITUTIONAL**: Operational language. Neutral Sans (Inter).
3.  **SYSTEMIC**: Telemetry, metrics. Monospace (JetBrains Mono).

### ARTICLE IV — SPATIAL & LAYOUT LAW
IRON Interfaces are **Chambers**, not pages.
Authorized motifs: Vertical sovereignty spines, sectional demarcation, anchored rails.
Prohibited: Floating card grids, masonry layouts, trend-driven hero sections.

### ARTICLE V — PANEL & COMPONENT LAW
Panels are Institutional Chambers. They must feel installed, governed, bounded.
Every chamber requires: Structural border, constitutional marker, internal depth.

### ARTICLE VI — MOTION & EFFECTS LAW
Motion is Mechanical, Not Emotional.
All motion must imply state transition or constitutional shift.
**Authorized**: Fades of authority, linear translations, structural reveals.
**Prohibited**: Delight, bounce, playfulness, brand animation.

### ARTICLE VII — PROHIBITIONS
Permanently Unconstitutional:
*   Bright UI themes
*   Consumer app tropes
*   Gamification aesthetics
*   Cheerful palettes
*   Emoji, mascots, illustrations
*   Glassmorphism

### ARTICLE VIII — TECHNICAL ENFORCEMENT
To ensure constitutional compliance, the codebase must adhere to:

#### VIII.1 Subscription Law
The UI must not "query" state; it must **subscribe** to it. All visual changes originate from the Institutional Adapter.

#### VIII.2 Variable Constitution
No component may define its own palette. It must consume:
*   `--iron-bg-*` (Infrastructure)
*   `--iron-signal-*` (State)
*   `--iron-font-*` (Jurisdiction)

#### VIII.3 Animation Trigger Law
Animations are illegal unless triggered by specific institutional events (e.g., `STANDING_CHANGED`, `BREACH_DECLARED`).

### ARTICLE IX — SOVEREIGN TEST
"If this were leaked, would it look like a private institutional system — or a startup product?"
If the answer is "product," it violates the constitution.
