# VERDICT SYSTEM CODE
## Law of Daily Judgment and Institutional Transition

### PREAMBLE
The Verdict is the mechanism by which Time is converted into History.
It is the only moment where Standing is permanently altered.

---

### ARTICLE I — THE VERDICT PIPELINE
Every Daily Verdict must strictly follow this execution sequence:

1.  **CLOCK CLOSURE**: The institutional day ends.
2.  **EVIDENCE GATHERING**: All obligations and inputs are collated.
3.  **ADJUDICATION**: The Engine determines Pass, Fail, or Breach.
4.  **STANDING RECALCULATION**: New Standing score and Band are derived.
5.  **LEDGER COMMIT**: The result is immutably written to history.
6.  **CEREMONY**: The result is presented to the user.

### ARTICLE II — THE FOUR VERDICTS
Only the following verdicts are legal outputs:

*   **COMPLETE (Pass)**: All obligations met. Standing maintained or increased.
*   **PARTIAL (Survival)**: Critical obligations met. No breach. Standing impact neutral/slight decay.
*   **FAILURE (Miss)**: Obligations unmet. Standing penalty applied. Risk increases.
*   **BREACH (Violation)**: Critical invariant violated. Immediate Standing Drop.

### ARTICLE III — CEREMONY LAW
The Verdict must not be a dashboard notification. It must be a blocking procedural event.
**User Exit Condition**: The user may not dismiss the verdict until the State Transition is acknowledged.

### ARTICLE IV — STATE TRANSITIONS
*   **On PASS**: Streak++ (Expand).
*   **On FAIL**: Streak-- (Decay) or Reset.
*   **On BREACH**: Streak = 0 (Fracture). Entering Recovery Mode.

### ARTICLE V — EVIDENCE REQUIREMENT
No Verdict may be issued without Evidence.
If data is missing, the system must default to **FAILURE** (Silence is not Compliance).
